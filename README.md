# ETH Master's Thesis--Bias Correction

During Feb 2026 - Aug 2026, the author completes her 28-week long Master's thesis under the co-supervision from ETH Seminar for Statistics (D-MATH) and ETH Institute for Atmosphere and Climate (D-USYS). 

The thesis addresses the impact of bias correction methods on regional climate model emulation and renewable energy prediction. Specific bias correction used are `CDFt` [1] and `R2D2` [2]. 

This repository contains the author's codes and results that generate the Master's thesis report. 

[1] Michelangeli, P.-A., M.Vrac, and H.Loukos (2009), Probabilistic downscaling approaches: Application to wind cumulative distribution functions, Geophys. Res. Lett., 36, L11708, doi:10.1029/2009GL038401.

[2] Vrac, M.: Multivariate bias adjustment of high-dimensional climate simulations: the Rank Resampling for Distributions and Dependences (R2D2) bias correction, Hydrol. Earth Syst. Sci., 22, 3175–3196, https://doi.org/10.5194/hess-22-3175-2018, 2018.
